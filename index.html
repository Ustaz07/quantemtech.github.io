BRIEF HISTORY OF GOMBE PRISON

The prison was established in the year 1919 with the capacity of 379 inmates by the emir of Gombe State Mallam Umaru while he was the emir in Council of Gombe Native authority. He migrated from Nafada in 1919 with the present Gombe Town. Gradually the Federal Government took over the administration of the prison in April 1968. Many things were change by the Federal Govrnment after they took over.

Key Functions Of The Nigeria Prison Service

Below are some of the major functions of this Paramilitary Commission In Nigeria:-
i.They have the mandate to take into lawful custody all those certified to be so kept by courts of competent jurisdiction;
ii. Produce suspects in courts as and when due;
iii. They Identify the causes of their anti-social dispositions;
iv. Set in motion mechanisms for their treatment and training for eventual reintegration into society as normal law abiding citizens on discharge; and
v. Administer Prisons Farms and Industries for this purpose and in the process generate revenue for the government.

Nigeria Prison Service rank structure:-
These includes:
1. Deputy Superintendent of Prisons (shortly called DSP)
2. Assistant Superintendent of Prisons I (the official abbreviation is ASP.I)
3. Assistant Superintendent of Prisons II (the official abbreviation is ASP.II)
4. Senior Inspector of Prisons (the quick name of this rank is SIP)
5. Inspector of Prisons (the simple abbreviation is IP)
6. Assistant Inspector of Prisons (the quick rank abbreviation is AIP) Prisons
7. Assistant I (shortly this rank is called PAI)
8. Prisons Assistant II (the rank’s simple abbreviation is PAII)
9. Prisons Assistant III (it is abbreviated as PAIII)
